

# ☕ Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard (Excel Project) – Feb 2026

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is an interactive Excel dashboard built using a Coffee Shop sales dataset. The objective of the project is to analyze sales performance, product trends, and revenue insights using advanced Excel functions and data visualization techniques.

The dashboard provides dynamic insights into sales performance, product analysis, and business trends using formulas such as **XLOOKUP** and **INDEX + MATCH**.

---

##❓ The Problem

"How can a coffee shop transition from 'gut-feeling' decisions to data-driven growth?"

While the shop was capturing transaction data, it lacked a centralized way to identify what was actually driving the business. I aimed to answer three critical questions:

Product Mix: Which 20% of products are driving 80% of the revenue?

Timing: When is the staff-to-customer ratio most critical (peak hours vs. dead zones)?

Customer Loyalty: Are our top five customers driving significant volume, or is the revenue spread thin across one-time visitors?

---

## 📂 The Data

The dataset consisted of raw transaction logs including Order IDs, Product Categories, and Customer Information.

Data Preparation & Cleaning:

Normalization: Used XLOOKUP and INDEX/MATCH to bridge disparate tables (Product IDs to Prices), ensuring a "single source of truth."

Sanitization: Handled missing values in customer fields and standardized date/time formats to allow for chronological trend analysis.

Feature Engineering: Created calculated columns for "Total Sales" and "Profit Margin" to move beyond simple volume metrics.

---

## 📊 The Insights

To visualize these findings, I built an interactive dashboard centered on three key pillars:

The 80/20 Product Rule: My analysis revealed that just three core products contribute to the majority of the total revenue. This suggests that menu complexity could be reduced to focus on high-margin favorites.

Morning Peak Velocity: By visualizing sales by the hour, I identified a significant revenue spike during morning hours.

Category Contribution: While "Coffee" is the volume leader, the "Product Type" analysis showed that certain secondary categories (like pastries or specialty beans) have higher individual profit margins despite lower volume.

---

## 💡 The Conclusion

Based on the dashboard insights, I recommend the following business actions:

Optimize Staffing: Increase floor coverage specifically during the morning peak (identified in the Trend Analysis) to reduce wait times and prevent "walk-outs."

Inventory Focus: Prioritize stock for the top 3 revenue-generating products to ensure zero stock-outs on high-velocity items.

Upsell Strategy: Since profit margins vary wildly across categories, the shop should implement a "bundle" promotion (e.g., Coffee + high-margin Pastry) to increase the average transaction value.
